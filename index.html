About

Brooke is a 20 year old professional climber from Boulder, CO who also happens to be one of my best friends. Her parents are Didier and Robyn Esbesfield-Raboutau, are former world champion rock climbers. Didier is a three time World Cup champion, and Robyn is a five time US champion and four time World Cup Champion.

Brooke attends University of San Diego but has taken a few semesters off to climb and train for the Olympics.

This year she went to her very first Olympics and competed in 3 different disciplines explained below.

Speed

  • Competitors race each other up a wall towering 50 feet with 31 holds on it.
  • The wall and its holds have been the same for the past 14 years.
  • The key is memorizing a path and mastering it.

The fastest men in the world can do this in less than six seconds; the fastest women, in less than seven; and most of the Tokyo competitors should be under 10.

Bouldering

Bouldering just uses climbing shoes and a bag of chalk over safety mats, your challenge is to climb short but tricky bouldering "problems" (a route, or sequence of moves) using balance, technique, strength, and your brain.
